a scroll poem

ama

a poem for my mother, inspired by when life gives you tangerines. written by iel.

listen while you scroll ↓

ama,

my mom once told me

she used to write poems in her college workbook,

before their house burned down, twice.

she cross-stitched a poem that spelled honey,

each letter framed in my grandparents’ home,

a confession of love to my dad.

she was editor-in-chief in elementary and high school,

top of her class for poetry,

a literary girl all on her own.

even when life grew precarious,

she found something to love in every moment.

may i live within my means,

and stay true to where i belong,

for any place i do not call home,

is one without your cooking.

— my ae-sun.

thank you for reading.

Design Rationale

This project uses the AOS (Animate On Scroll) JavaScript library to transform my personal poem about my mother into an interactive, scroll-based narrative. I chose AOS because it allowed me to stylize and pace the content without adding unnecessary complexity. By experimenting with the timing of the fade animations, I was able to mirror the feeling of an intimate conversation unfolding gradually, rather than presenting the poem all at once.

Through this proof-of-concept, I wanted to demonstrate how lightweight animation can give static text greater emotional weight. The progressive reveal helps orchestrate the poem in a way that feels coherent and intentional, encouraging the reader to slow down and move through it thoughtfully. Beyond this implementation, the same approach could be expanded into larger multimedia experiences. My initial idea was to create a children’s picture book with illustrations and text fading in as you scroll, but ultimately this personal poem resonated with me more and felt like a more meaningful use of the plugin.
